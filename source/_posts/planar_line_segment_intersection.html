--- title: Determine if Two Planar Line Segments Intersect date: 2018-10-13 hidden: false mathjax: true tags: ---

Problem

Given two line segments defined by points: \(AB\) and \(CD\), check whether two segments intersect.

1. Intersection of a line and line segment

Given line \(L\), line segment \(AB\) and its correspondent line \(L_{AB}\). The intersection \(K\) of \(L\) and \(L_{AB}\). Then point \(K\) can be expressed as:

\[ K = A + t \overrightarrow{AB} \tag{1-1} \]

Here is the relationship between \(t\) and \(L\), \(AB\):

\[ \begin{cases} L \ \text{intersects} \ AB, & t \in (0, 1) \\ L \ \text{touches} \ AB, & t = 0 \ \text{or} \ t = 1 \\ L \ \text{misses} \ AB, & t \in (-\infty, 0) \cup (1, \infty) \end{cases} \]

Therefore, the sufficient and necessary condition of \(L\) intersecting \(AB\) is:

\[ K = A + t \overrightarrow{AB}, \ t \in (0, 1) \tag{1-2} \]

2. Lemma A. Determine if Two Line Segments Intersect

Given line segments \(AB\), \(CD\) and their correspondent line \(L_{AB}\), \(L_{CD}\). It is obvious if and only if \(L_{AB}\) intersects \(CD\) and \(L_{CD}\) intersects \(AB\), these two line segments intersect.

A Rapid Method

Line segments \(AB\) intersects \(CD\) if and only if:

\[ (\overrightarrow{AC} \times \overrightarrow{AB}) \cdot (\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AD}) > 0 \\ \text{and} \\ (\overrightarrow{CA} \times \overrightarrow{CD}) \cdot (\overrightarrow{CD} \times \overrightarrow{CB}) > 0 \tag{2-1} \]

Proof:

According to formula (1-2)

\[ m \overrightarrow{AB} = \overrightarrow{AK} = \overrightarrow{AC} + t \overrightarrow{CD} = \overrightarrow{AD} + (1-t) \overrightarrow{DC} \tag{2-2} \]

\(m\) is random none-zero constant. Then we have:

\[ \overrightarrow{AB} = \frac{1}{m}(\overrightarrow{AC} + t \overrightarrow{CD}) \tag{2-3} \] \[ \overrightarrow{AB} = \frac{1}{m}(\overrightarrow{AD} + t \overrightarrow{DC}) \tag{2-4} \]

Then the first term of (2-1) becomes:

\[ \frac{t(1-t)}{m^2} (\overrightarrow{AC} \times \overrightarrow{CD}) \cdot (\overrightarrow{DC} \times \overrightarrow{AD}) > 0 \tag{2-5} \]

For random triangle \(ACD\), \((\overrightarrow{AC} \times \overrightarrow{CD}) \cdot (\overrightarrow{DC} \times \overrightarrow{AD}) > 0\). Therefore, formula (2-5) becomes:

\[ \frac{t(1-t)}{m^2} > 0 \]

Becasue \(m^2 > 0\),

\[ t(1-t) > 0 \] \[ 0 < t < 1 \tag{2-6} \]

Therefore, the first term of formula (2-1) means \(L_{AB}\) intersects \(CD\). Similarly, the second term means \(L_{CD}\) intersects \(AB\). According to Lemma A., \(AB\) intersects \(CD\).
