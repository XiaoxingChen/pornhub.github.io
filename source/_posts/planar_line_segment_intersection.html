--- title: Planar Line Segment Intersection date: 2018-10-13 hidden: true mathjax: true tags: ---

Problem

Given two line segments defined by points: \(P_1, P_2, O, A\), check whether two segments intersect.

Lemma 1. Check two points at the same side of a segment

Given line Segment \(OA\) and points \(P_1, P_2\), whether the points locate at the same side of the vector can be judged by:

\[ (\overrightarrow{OA} \times \overrightarrow{OP_1}) \cdot (\overrightarrow{OA} \times \overrightarrow{OP_2}) > 0 \]

Lemma 2. If both of the segments' end points locate at the different sides of each other, the two segments intersect.

Conclusion

The necessary and sufficient conditions of two line segment intersect is:

\[ (\overrightarrow{OA} \times \overrightarrow{OP_1}) \cdot (\overrightarrow{OA} \times \overrightarrow{OP_2}) > 0 \]

and

\[ (\overrightarrow{P_1 P_2} \times \overrightarrow{P_1 O}) \cdot (\overrightarrow{P_1 P_2} \times \overrightarrow{P_1 A}) > 0 \]
